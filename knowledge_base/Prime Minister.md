#### **Council of Ministers to Aid and Advice the President**

**Article:** (1)
- **Purpose**: To assist and advise the President in exercising his functions.
- **Limitations**: The President may require the Council of Ministers to reconsider their advice, either generally or otherwise, and the President shall act in accordance with the advice tendered after such reconsideration.
- **Disqualifications**: Members of either House of Parliament belonging to any political party who are disqualified for being a member of that House under Article 109 shall also be disqualified to be appointed as a Minister under this Article for duration of the period commencing from the date of his disqualification till the date on which the term of his office as such member would expire or where he contests any election to either House of Parliament before the expiry of such period, till the date on which he is declared elected, whichever is earlier.

**Article:** (2)
- **Total Number of Ministers**: The Prime Minister shall be appointed by the President and the other Ministers shall be appointed by the President on the advice of the Prime Minister.
- **Reconsideration Period**: The question whether any, and if so what, advice was tendered by Ministers to the President shall not be inquired into in any court.

**Article:** (3)
- **Constitutional Limitations**: The Council of Ministers shall be collectively responsible to the House of the People.
- **Appointment**: The Prime Minister shall be appointed by the President and the other Ministers shall be appointed by the President on the advice of the Prime Minister.
- **Term of Office**: The Ministers shall hold office during the pleasure of the President.

**Article:** (4)
- **Salary and Allowances**: The salaries and allowances of Ministers shall be such as Parliament may from time to time by law determine and, until Parliament so determines, shall be as specified in the Second Schedule.

**Article:** (5)
- **Orders and Instruments**: The President shall make rules for the more convenient transaction of the business of the Government of India, and for the allocation among Ministers of the said business.
- **Validity**: The validity of an order or instrument which is so authenticated shall not be called in question on the ground that it is not an order or instrument made or executed by the President.

**Article:** (6)
- **Resignation**: The salaries and allowances of Ministers shall be such as Parliament may from time to time by law determine and, until Parliament so determines, shall be as specified in the Second Schedule.

#### **Conduct of Business of the Government of India**

**Article:** 
- **All Executive Action**: All executive action of the Government of India shall be expressed to be taken in the name of the President.
- **Order and Instrument**: Orders and other instruments made and executed in the name of the President shall be authenticated in such manner as may be specified in rules to be made by the President, and the validity of an order or instrument which is so authenticated shall not be called in question on the ground that it is not an order or instrument made or executed by the President.
- **Vice-Presidents**: The Vice-President of India shall be the Chairman of the Rajya Sabha.

### Notes:

#### **Duties of Prime Minister as Respectively Concerning Filing of Information to the President, etc.**

**Article:** 
- **Filing of Information**: It shall be the duty of the Prime Minister to communicate to the President all decisions of the Council of Ministers relating to the administration of the affairs of the Union and proposals for legislation;
- **Filing of Information**: The Prime Minister shall furnish such information relating to the administration of the affairs of the Union and proposals for legislation as the President may call for; and
- **Filing of Information**: If the President so requires, the Prime Minister shall submit for the consideration of the Council of Ministers any matter on which a decision has been taken by a Minister but which has not been considered by the Council.

#### **Role of the Vice-Presidents of India as the Chairman of the Rajya Sabha**

**Article:** 
- **Chairman of the Rajya Sabha**: The Vice-President of India shall be the Chairman of the Rajya Sabha.
